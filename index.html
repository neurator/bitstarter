Everyone has objects that they wish to repair. Finding honest, skillful repair technicians is difficult. Even when a good fixit person is available, parts and labor are sometimes prohibitively expensive. Many objects collect dust as forgotten projects, or are sent away to the trash pile. mendUP seeks to connect people with those who have the skills to fix these objects. Less trash, more goods, stronger communities.

mendUP seeks to be a platform. A community network, where menders reveal their skillset to the public. Seekers of repair can browse offerings, and if they see no offers they can post a request for repair. Mendable objects can be of all types. Dress shirts, computers, automobiles, vintage cabinets, musical instruments; you get the idea. A rating system will ensure that the quality of repair is high. As things develop, mendUP will offer photo recognition for objects that are difficult to identify. A search engine will help users (seekers and menders alike) find affordable parts and links to potential fabricators if the part is discontinued. Plus the app will tell the user if the cost of repair is beyond the cost of replacement, and will guide the user to a broken-goods marketplace. Utilizing this marketplace, enterprising menders can maintain part databases on the site so future seekers need not trash their broken items. Finally, a menders wiki will offer DIY guides and links to schematics.

FAQ

How are you going to compete with pre-existing Ecommerce for parts?
By connecting customers to menders directly, the supply chain becomes localized. A greater sense of trust arises. Current part sites will still be useful, but the goal here is to enable in-person transactions and trust networks.
How will you make money?
Menders who offer services will pay a small fee. Seekers pay nothing, as we wish to encourage people to request repair. mendUP could eventually higher its own repair staff to fill gaps in communities where certain high demand repairs are unavailable, and/or offer certificate training. Selling repair specific ads is a possibility as well. Perhaps mendUP will eventually offer its own part supply or fabrication facilities. Its up to consumer demand to see where the future focus will lie.
